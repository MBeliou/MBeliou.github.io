Why I Chose Vuejs For All My Projects

Jan 22, 2019
3 min read

Coming to Javascript from other languages such as Python and Golang, I was a bit overwhelmed by the bad press it was getting, both from its well known gotchas and from the amount of ever-changing frameworks in existence.

I started out learning vanilla JS and JQuery from w3schools and the Eloquent Javascript book (Here’s the online version of it), which gave me a fair understanding of the language but a somewhat shaky foundation.

I then began to take a look at what I’d call the 3 big frameworks a few google searches revealed to me: Angular, React and Vue.

Angular

I had already heard about Angular from a few years before from an old classmate.

I vividly remembered him telling me about how complex it was but the promises of Angular 2 and the use of Typescript had me going through the tutorial in no time.

Angular seemed quite verbose but quite logical and I decided to roll with it and got myself Angular in Action, which goes further than the tutorial, with a good balance between explanations, examples and best practices.

Well armed with the knowledge of this book, I decided to use Angular for a few unsupervised toy projects and I quickly came to the realization that using Angular beyond any simple use seemed to require a very thought out design that I was somewhat still unable to come up with given my inexperience.

Not wanting yet to sink a considerable amount of time trialing, I decided to take a look at the other frameworks I listed on top of this post.

React

React was sort of the natural next choice. It seemed to be the cool kid around that every startup was using.

Following the same process as Angular, I toured the tutorial and made a few simple pet projects in order to understand it.

I came to grow fond of the component based design but JSX ended up being a deal breaker for me as I just plainly disliked it.

I also tried using Typescript while developing in React which turned out to be a big mistake and the source of endless frustrations until I just dropped it and went back to plain old JS.

Vue

I then checked out Vue, which, after a short tour of the homepage, seemed very similar to React.

I half reluctantly went through the docs which got my interest going and, as before, tried out a few simple projects which actually got me quite hooked.

It seemed to solve so easily most of my issues with both Angular AND React: most of the rookie mistakes I made while coding using Vue were actually solved by taking some time to think and going through the docs.

In order to get a few pointers and kickstart my use of the framework, I used this book. It has its issues but the code is freely available on github and has the added benefit of guiding a new user (like I was) into firebase.

Closing Words

Thanks to its simplicity and efficiency, I am only using Vue for everything in the browser.

After going through the initial learning pains and beginner mistakes for a few days at most, I feel like Vue just works. I can just easily play around with my code and it feels fast and simple.

And that’s what matters to me.
